**Yoga** is a holistic practice that integrates physical postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation to promote physical, mental, and spiritual well-being. 

**Origins:**
- Originated in ancient India around 2,500-500 BC.
- Derived from the ancient Indian philosophy of Vedanta.

**Goals:**
- To achieve unity of mind and body.
- To reduce physical and mental stress.
- To foster self-awareness and compassion.
- To spiritual growth and transformation.

**Components:**

**1. Asana (Physical Postures)**
- Sequences of postures that improve flexibility, strength, and balance.
- Connect the body with the breath.

**2. Pranayama (Breathing Techniques)**
- Techniques for controlling and manipulating the breath.
- Improves concentration and oxygenation.

**3. Meditation**
- Practice for cultivating inner calmness and awareness.
- Develops focus and concentration.

**4. Yoga Philosophy**
- Ethical guidelines and philosophical underpinnings of yoga.
- Teaches about self, reality, and the relationship between mind and body.

**Types of Yoga:**
- Hatha Yoga
- Ashtanga Yoga
- Vinyasa Yoga
- Iyengar Yoga
- Kundalini Yoga

**Benefits:**

- Increased flexibility and mobility
- Reduced stress and anxiety
- Improved strength and balance
- Enhanced respiratory function
- Increased mindfulness and self-awareness
- Reduced pain and stiffness
- Spiritual growth and transformation

**Principles of Yoga:**

- **Satya (Truth)**: Practicing with integrity.
- **Asteya (Non-stealing)**: Respect for oneself and others.
- **Karuna (Compassion)**: Treating the body and mind with kindness.
- **Aparigraha (Non-attachment)**: Letting go of material possessions and desires.

**Benefits for Society:**
- Promotes physical and mental health.
- Reduces healthcare costs.
- Increases productivity and creativity.
- Boosts community resilience.
