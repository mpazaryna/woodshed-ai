**Yoga** is a physical, mental, and spiritual practice that originated in ancient India. It combines physical postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation to promote physical and mental well-being.

**Physical Practice (Asanas)**
- Sequence of postures that improve flexibility, strength, and balance.
- Enhances physical fitness and reduces stress and tension.

**Breathing Practices (Pranayama)**
- Techniques to control and expand the breath.
- Improves lung capacity, reduces stress, and enhances meditation.

**Meditation**
- Stillness and awareness practice.
- Reduces worry, anxiety, and depression.
- Promotes inner peace and spiritual growth.

**Origins and Philosophy:**

- Yoga is rooted in the ancient Indian philosophies of Hinduism and Buddhism.
- The word "yoga" comes from the Sanskrit root "yuj," meaning "to yoke" or "to unite."
- It suggests a union between the mind and body.

**Types of Yoga:**

- Hatha yoga: Focuses on physical postures and breathing.
- Hatha Vinyasa: Dynamic flow of postures.
- Vinyasa yoga: Continuous movement through various poses.
- Restorative yoga: Gentle poses to ease tension and promote relaxation.
- Kundalini yoga: Includes physical postures, breathing, and meditation to awaken energy in the body.

**Benefits of Yoga:**

- Increased flexibility and strength
- Reduced stress and anxiety
- Improved breathing
- Reduced pain and stiffness
- Enhanced mindfulness and focus
- Spiritual growth

**Who can practice Yoga?**

Yoga is suitable for people of all ages and fitness levels. Modifications can be made to accommodate individual needs.

**Benefits for Society:**

- Yoga promotes physical and mental health in individuals.
- It can reduce healthcare costs and improve productivity.
- It fosters a sense of community and promotes spiritual values.
